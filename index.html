Meta-Analysis of Landslide Disasters in India

Landslides are one of the most devastating natural hazards, causing significant loss of life, property, and infrastructure, particularly in mountainous and hilly regions. In India, the risk of landslides is heightened due to diverse geographical and climatic conditions, as well as human activities like deforestation, unplanned construction, and mining. The country's vulnerability is especially pronounced in the Himalayan region, the Western Ghats, and parts of the Northeastern states, which experience high levels of rainfall, seismic activity, and steep topography, making them particularly susceptible to landslides.
